Core claim: Every system compresses. If every system compresses, intelligence must be compression-aware to be real.

What is CAI

Compression-Aware Intelligence (CAI) is a framework for recognizing, measuring, and leveraging the distortions that arise from compressing information, whether in human cognition, AI models, or collective narratives, with the goal of improving reliability, accountability, and authenticity in intelligence systems.

  • Locate compression sites
  • Score the loss
  • Map contradictions
  • Attach provenance
  • Enable audit

Why it matters

  • Fewer hallucinations
  • Clearer failure modes
  • Safer tradeoffs
  • Better accountability

If you can point to the compression, you can reason about the error.

At a glance

  • MirrorNet: Reflects compression choices to the user and the model.
  • Hallucinet: Detects risk tied to hidden compression.
  • CAC tests: Cost-aware checks for evaluations and QA.
  • Contradiction maps: Find, score, and fix conflicts made worse by compression.
