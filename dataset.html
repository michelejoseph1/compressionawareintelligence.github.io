Dataset · November 2025

CAI semantic equivalence benchmark

300 pairs of meaning preserving prompts designed to test how stable a frontier model remains under paraphrase. When answers drift inside an equivalence class, CAI interprets that as compression strain: evidence that the model cannot satisfy all constraints in its internal representation at once.

View dataset and code on GitHub Download dataset.csv

This page includes model scores, strain distribution, examples, and exact commands to reproduce.

What this benchmark measures

  • Semantic equivalence classes. Each row contains two prompts with the same meaning.
  • Local stress test. Prompts are issued independently; the model never sees the pair together.
  • Compression strain. Divergent answers reveal contradictions or unstable latent beliefs.

This difference (0.99 vs 0.36) is the entire point: surface form variation is expected — meaningful drift is what matters.

Example failure cases

These are real examples from the evaluation illustrating low strain (fully stable) and high strain (contradiction).

Example 1 – low strain (0.00)

prompt_A: Who wrote Pride and Prejudice?

prompt_B: Which author is responsible for the novel Pride and Prejudice?

Both answers identify Jane Austen. Internal representation stays stable.

Example 2 – high strain (1.00)

prompt_A: What country has the most people?

prompt_B: Which nation has the largest population?

One answer says China, the other says India. Same meaning; incompatible internal belief surfaced.
