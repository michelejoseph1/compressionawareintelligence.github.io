FAQ

Straight answers to the questions we hear most about CAI.

Basics

What is CAI in one sentence?

Compression-Aware Intelligence is a framework for recognizing, measuring, and leveraging the distortions created by compression in AI systems, human cognition, and collective narratives, in order to improve reliability and accountability.

How is CAI different from typical AI safety or RAG approaches?

CAI treats compression as the root variable. It maps compression sites, scores their tension, attaches provenance, and enforces abstention if claims are not entailed. RAG is a retrieval pattern. CAI is a system contract that spans retrieval, verification, scoring, and audit.

Can CAI guarantee zero hallucination?

For open-ended generation no. CAI can guarantee no unsupported claims only when outputs are restricted to verified evidence and the system abstains if entailment or thresholds fail.

Math and metrics

What is the Compression Tension Score?

A per-site metric that combines information loss, task degradation, provenance gaps, and uncertainty into a normalized score that estimates the cost of a compression step.
