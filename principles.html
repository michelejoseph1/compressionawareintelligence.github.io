Principles of Compression-Aware Intelligence

If an AI system cannot meet the principles below, it cannot claim to be reliable.

1. Name the compression

Every system compresses. If we cannot point to the step where it happens, we cannot reason about the error it creates.

2. Score the loss

Each compression has a cost. Measure it, weight it, and show it. Reliability starts with visible trade-offs.

3. Mirror the illusion

Outputs look seamless, but they are stitched from compressed paths. Showing that chain lowers false trust and raises awareness.

4. Map contradictions

Conflicts are not errors to hide but signals to track. Contradictions show where compression has bent truth out of shape.

5. Provenance first

Every output must carry its receipts. A short record of sources and steps is not extra; it is essential.

6. Audit by default

Reliability means replay. A claim is only as strong as the ease with which a third party can retrace it.
