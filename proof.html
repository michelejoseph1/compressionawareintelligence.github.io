Proof every system compresses

This is not a formal theorem. It's a testable scaffold, enough to guide practice.

Axiom 1. Compression is forced by limits

Finite compute, memory, and bandwidth mean no system can preserve every detail. All must reduce.

  • Biology: the retina and cortex filter raw input.
  • Law: many cases collapse into rules and thresholds.
  • Markets: prices condense distributed beliefs.
  • Software: APIs hide internal complexity.

Axiom 2. Prediction relies on compressed form

To predict is to summarize. Models and minds both store compressed patterns to act quickly.

  • Science: equations distill observations.
  • Machine learning: parameters encode training data.
  • Planning: heuristics compress search spaces.

Corollary. Hidden compression hides error

When compression is implicit, contradictions and rare cases vanish from view. The result is illusion: fluent output that conceals brittleness. Make compression explicit, and errors become visible, measurable, and fixable.

Cross-checks across domains

  • Information theory: MDL links learning and compression.
  • Neuroscience: sparse and predictive coding.
  • Policy: metrics compress reality into scores.

The testable claim

Teams that list and score compression sites ship more reliable systems than those that ignore them. CAI provides the checklist and the scores.

If every system compresses, then real intelligence must be compression-aware.
