Proof every system compresses

This is a clear, testable scaffold. It is not a formal theorem. It is enough to guide practice.

Axiom 1. Finite resources force compression

  • Compute. Memory, time, and bandwidth are limited. Systems must reduce detail.
  • Biology. The retina and cortex filter and group raw input.
  • Law. Many cases become rules, tests, and thresholds.
  • Markets. Prices compress distributed beliefs.

Axiom 2. Prediction uses compressed form

  • Science. Equations compress observations.
  • ML. Parameters compress training data.
  • Planning. Heuristics compress search.

Corollary. Hidden compression hides error

When compression is implicit, contradictions and rare cases vanish from view. This creates illusions and brittle behavior. Make compression explicit and the errors become visible and fixable.

Cross domain checks

  • Information theory. MDL links learning and compression.
  • Neuroscience. Sparse and predictive coding.
  • Software. APIs compress internal complexity.
  • Policy. Impact metrics compress reality.

Testable claim

Teams that list and score compression sites ship more reliable systems than teams that ignore them. CAI provides the checklist and the scores.
