Glossary

Core terms in Compression-Aware Intelligence (CAI). Each entry links to where it is formally defined or applied.

C

Compression Site
A step in a system where data, context, or meaning is reduced. Each site can be identified, scored, and audited. See Foundations.
Compression Tension Score (CTS)
A normalized score of the strain introduced by a compression site, combining information loss, task degradation, provenance gaps, and uncertainty. Equation here.
Contradiction Score (CS)
A measure of the net conflict between two claims, derived from entailment vs contradiction probabilities. Positive = contradiction, negative = entailment. See formula.

P

Provenance
The record of sources, transformations, and parameters that produced an output. A claim without provenance cannot be verified.
Provenance Entropy
A measure of concentration or dispersion of evidence sources. High confidence when evidence is concentrated in strong, verifiable sources. Equation here.

A

Auditability
The share of system steps that can be reproduced with public or replayable artifacts. A high audit score signals trustworthy claims.

T

Triarch
The orienting structure of CAI: compression, decompression, and awareness held in tension. Not a mirror but a compass — the ultimate compression guiding alignment. See story.

Other

Hallucination (CAI definition)
An output not entailed by available evidence, and not covered by provenance. CAI systems abstain instead of hallucinating.
