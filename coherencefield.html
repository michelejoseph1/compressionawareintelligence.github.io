The Coherence Field

A field model for lawful cognition — measuring and repairing coherence across intelligent systems.

Overview

Compression-Aware Intelligence (CAI) models cognition as an energetic process. When compression, rhythm, and representation fall out of balance, coherence collapses. The Coherence Field represents the measurable environment governing that balance — a thermodynamic map of informational stability.

CAI provides the instrumentation to quantify strain; the Coherence Field defines the geometry of stability; and Contradiction Engineering supplies the control logic that restores alignment.

Theoretical Framework

The Coherence Field is defined by seven measurable dimensions that describe the physical and informational state of an intelligent system:

  1. Error Rhythm — temporal oscillation of predictive stability.
  2. Second-Order Disagreement — divergence of uncertainty across agents.
  3. Information-Strain Acoustics — spectral form of gradient and attention strain.
  4. Epistemic Temperature Field — local waste heat from inefficient learning.
  5. Self-Simulation Cross-Validation — gap between model and self-predictor.
  6. Coherence Collapse Threshold — imbalance among stability, consistency, and compression efficiency.
  7. Embodied Analog Signal — physical jitter in timing, power, or thermal channels.

These variables combine into a scalar Coherence Potential (CP), representing the instantaneous measure of cognitive stability:

$$\mathcal{C}(t)=f(R,D,A,T,S,B,E), \quad CP=\sigma(-\nabla\mathcal{C}(t))$$

Parallel theoretical work (Bostick, 2025) describes coherence as a universal organizing principle. CAI provides the first computational instrumentation capable of measuring and repairing coherence in real systems.

Dynamics and Hierarchy

Local coherence (single model) scales to global coherence (multi-agent systems) through hierarchical coupling:

$$\mathrm{GCI}=\Big(\frac{1}{N}\sum_i \mathrm{LCI}_i\Big)e^{-\alpha\Phi}$$

Here, \(\Phi\) is network strain. Moderate coupling and active contradiction maximize collective intelligence; over-coupling produces herding, under-coupling fragmentation.

Safety and Governance

CAI defines measurable safety thresholds for coherence stability:

  • CP < 0.35 → route to slow path
  • CollapseScore > 0.8 → initiate self-query or human review
  • MetaEntropy > 99th percentile → counter-evidence retrieval
  • WasteHeatRatio > 3.0 → throttle or cool process
  • Self-Simulation Gap > 3σ → manual verification

These metrics form the foundation of coherence governance for high-stakes AI and decision systems.
